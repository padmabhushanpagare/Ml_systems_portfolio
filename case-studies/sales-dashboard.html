● Executive Summary

The sales organization was crippled by fragmented data across CRM (Salesforce) and ERP (NetSuite) systems, resulting in "Excel hell" during quarterly reviews. I architected a centralized data warehouse using Snowflake and dbt to unify these sources, feeding a real-time Tableau dashboard. The solution saved 20+ hours/week of analyst time and exposed critical conversion bottlenecks, leading to a 10% reallocation of marketing spend.

Business Problem

The VP of Sales lacked trust in the "Weekly Business Review" (WBR) deck because the numbers rarely matched the finance department's reports. Sales Operations spent 2 days every week manually merging CSV exports to calculate basic metrics like Pipeline Coverage and Win Rates. The organization needed a Single Source of Truth that was automated, audited, and accessible to non-technical regional directors.

Data & Modeling Strategy

The core challenge was reconciling differing definitions of "Revenue" between Sales (Bookings) and Finance (Recognized Revenue).

Visualization & KPI Selection

We moved beyond vanity metrics (e.g., "Total Leads") to actionable efficiency indicators. The dashboard was designed with a drill-down architecture: Global View → Regional View → Rep Performance → Opportunity Details.

  • Pipeline Coverage Ratio: (Pipeline Amount / Quota Remaining). Helps identify regions at risk of missing quarter targets early.
  • Sales Cycle Velocity: Days from Opportunity Creation to Closed-Won. Used to benchmark rep efficiency.
  • Push Rate: Percentage of deals pushed from one quarter to the next, indicating poor qualification hygiene.

Key Insights & Decision Impact

Once the data was visible, several operational inefficiencies became obvious:

  1. The EMEA Lag: The dashboard revealed that Enterprise deals in EMEA had a 2.1x longer sales cycle than in North America.
    Decision: Regional leadership adjusted EMEA quotas to account for longer ramp times, reducing rep turnover.
  2. Lead Source Quality: "Paid Social" leads had high volume but a <1% win rate compared to "Webinar" leads (8%).
    Decision: Marketing reallocated $50k/quarter from Social to Webinar production.

Performance & Deployment

To ensure adoption, performance was paramount:

  • Data Engineering: We utilized incremental models in dbt to only process new records daily, keeping build times under 15 minutes.
  • Dashboard Performance: We used Tableau Extracts rather than Live connections to ensure sub-3-second load times for complex aggregations.
  • Security: Implemented Row-Level Security (RLS) so Account Executives could only view their own pipeline, while Directors saw their full region.
