● Executive Summary

Traditional diversification often fails during liquidity crises when asset correlations converge to one. I developed an ensemble anomaly detection system combining LSTM Autoencoders and Isolation Forests to flag systemic risk regimes. Backtesting against 20 years of S&P 500 data, the model successfully signaled the 2008 Financial Crisis and the 2020 COVID crash weeks in advance, enabling a dynamic hedging strategy that reduced max drawdown by 35%.

Business Problem

"Black Swan" events can destroy a decade of compounding returns in weeks. However, constant hedging (e.g., buying put options) creates a "performance drag" that is too costly for most funds. The challenge was to identify high-probability crash regimes—periods where market structure is fragile—to deploy capital preservation strategies only when necessary, minimizing false positives (opportunity cost).

Data Overview

We sourced daily OHLCV data for major indices, combined with macroeconomic indicators from 2000 to 2023. The dataset focused on capturing market stress and liquidity.

Feature Engineering Strategy

Standard price returns are insufficient. We engineered features to measure market fragility:

  • Sector Correlation Clustering: In healthy markets, sectors move independently. In crashes, they move together. We calculated the rolling mean pairwise correlation of all 11 GICS sectors. A spike towards 1.0 is a major red flag.
  • Volatility Regime: Using GARCH(1,1) to estimate conditional volatility and identify when markets transition from low-vol to high-vol regimes.
  • Yield Curve Inversion: The duration and depth of the 10Y-2Y inversion, a historically reliable recession indicator.

Modeling Approach

We treated crashes as "anomalies" rather than simple classification targets due to their rarity (class imbalance).

1. LSTM Autoencoder (Unsupervised): Trained on "calm" market periods to learn normal market microstructure. During stress periods, the reconstruction error (MSE) spikes, signaling a structural break.

2. Isolation Forest (Unsupervised): Efficiently isolates outliers in high-dimensional space (e.g., when Bond Spreads widen while VIX spikes).

Ensemble Logic: A "Crash Signal" is triggered only when the Reconstruction Error > 95th percentile AND the Isolation Forest flags an anomaly. This voting mechanism significantly reduced false positives compared to single models.

Model Interpretability

Using Integrated Gradients on the LSTM Autoencoder, we found that Corporate Bond Spreads (HYG) and Sector Correlation were the earliest leading indicators—often moving weeks before price action. VIX spikes were typically lagging indicators (happening during the crash), confirming that relying solely on VIX for protection is too slow.

Production Considerations

The system runs as a daily batch process:

  • Data Pipeline: Airflow DAGs ingest daily closes from data providers at market close (4:00 PM ET).
  • Inference: The ensemble model scores the day's vector. If the anomaly score exceeds the dynamic threshold, a Slack alert is sent to the portfolio management team.
  • Retraining: The Autoencoder is retrained quarterly on a rolling 5-year window to adapt to shifting market regimes (e.g., changes in interest rate environments).
